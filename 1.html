Origin of Hangul

Did you know that Hangul is one of the easiest letters to learn? According to Britannica , Korean alphabets, called Hangul, were officially introduced in 1446 by King Sejong, the fourth king of the Choson dynasty. King Sejong had found the need to promote literacy for common people in order to help them read, write and understand. Therefore, he developed a new unique alphabet system based on Korean pronunciations, which was proclaimed to be Hunminjeongum (original name).

Fun Fact

Hangul Day is on every 9th of October, when we commemorate the creation of Hangul.
